üìñ What is Passive Voice?

Passive voice is a way of constructing sentences where the object of the action becomes the subject of the sentence. Instead of focusing on WHO does the action (the agent), we focus on WHAT is done or WHO receives the action. The emphasis shifts from the doer to the receiver.

üîÑ Active vs Passive Voice

üî¥ Active Voice

Focus: WHO does the action
Subject performs action
Subject + Verb + Object
"The cat ate the fish."
(Focus: THE CAT)

üü¢ Passive Voice

Focus: WHAT is done / WHO receives
Subject receives action
Subject + TO BE + Past Participle + BY (agent)
"The fish was eaten by the cat."
(Focus: THE FISH)

‚öôÔ∏è Formation: Passive Voice Structure

üìê AUXILIARY VERB (TO BE) + PAST PARTICIPLE (V3)

The form of "to be" changes depending on the tense. The main verb stays in Past Participle form (V3).

üìù Passive Voice in Different Tenses

Present Simple Passive

Structure: AM/IS/ARE + V3
Example: "Dinner is prepared by my mother."
Active: My mother prepares dinner.

Past Simple Passive

Structure: WAS/WERE + V3
Example: "The house was built in 1990."
Active: They built the house in 1990.

Future Simple Passive

Structure: WILL BE + V3
Example: "The project will be completed next week."
Active: They will complete the project next week.

Present Perfect Passive

Structure: HAVE/HAS BEEN + V3
Example: "The letter has been sent."
Active: Someone has sent the letter.

Present Continuous Passive

Structure: AM/IS/ARE BEING + V3
Example: "The cake is being prepared right now."
Active: Someone is preparing the cake right now.

üéØ When to Use Passive Voice

Situation Example
1. Agent is unknown or unimportant "My car was stolen." (We don't know/don't care who stole it)
2. Focus on the action or result "The bridge was completed in 2020." (Focus: completion, not builder)
3. Formal or official writing "It is recommended that students arrive early." (Scientific/formal)
4. Avoiding blame or responsibility "Mistakes were made." (Not saying WHO made them)
5. News, reports, academic writing "A new discovery was announced yesterday." (News style)
6. General knowledge or universal truths "English is spoken by millions." (Known fact)

‚úì General knowledge, focus on action

‚ö° Active vs Passive: Complete Comparison

Focus WHO does the action WHAT is done / WHO receives
Subject Performs the action Receives the action

üí° Important Notes

‚≠ê BY AGENT (optional):

You can include or omit the agent (the person/thing doing the action):
"The cake was made by Sarah." (agent mentioned)
"The cake was made." (agent omitted)

Omit the agent if: unknown, unimportant, or obvious
‚≠ê COMMON MISTAKES:

‚ùå "The house is built." (passive - sounds incomplete)
‚úì "The house is being built." (currently happening)
‚úì "The house was built." (past, now complete)

‚ùå "I am interested by this book." (wrong preposition)
‚úì "I am interested in this book." (correct - not passive, adjective)
‚≠ê ACTIVE IS USUALLY BETTER:

Active voice is clearer and more direct. Use passive voice only when:
‚Ä¢ Agent is unknown
‚Ä¢ Focus on the action/result
‚Ä¢ Formal writing required
‚Ä¢ Professional or scientific context
‚≠ê VERBS THAT DON'T USE PASSIVE:

‚Ä¢ Intransitive verbs (no object): go, come, happen
‚Ä¢ "He went to school." ‚ùå "School was gone to by him."
‚Ä¢ Stative verbs: have, be, seem, appear (in certain uses)
