üìñ What is Past Perfect?

The Past Perfect (also called Pluperfect) is used to describe an action that happened BEFORE another action in the past. It shows which action happened first when we're talking about two past events.

‚úÖ Positive:

They had left before we arrived
‚ùå Negative:

‚ùì Question:

Had they left? No, they hadn't.

üéØ When to Use Past Perfect

Usage Example
1. Earlier past action before a later past action After I had studied, I went to bed.
2. Cause and effect in the past Because she had forgotten the keys, we couldn't enter.
3. Showing experience up to a point in the past By 2020, I had visited 15 countries.
4. Reported speech (he said, she told, etc.) He said he had completed the project.
