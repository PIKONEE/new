The Past Continuous (also called Past Progressive) is used to describe an action that was happening at a specific time in the past. The action was ongoing or in progress when something else happened.

üéØ When to Use Past Continuous

Usage Example
1. Action at a specific time in the past At 3 o'clock, I was studying.
2. Two actions: long action (PC) interrupted by short action (PS) I was sleeping WHEN the phone rang.
3. Two parallel actions happening simultaneously While I was cooking, she was setting the table.
4. Setting the scene/background in a story The sun was shining, birds were singing.

‚úì A long action interrupted by a short action
